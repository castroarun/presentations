The first fifteen minutes tell you where the day is going.

Most traders watch the open and react. The algorithm waits exactly fifteen minutes, marks the high and the low, then commits — only when price escapes that range does it act. Watch it work, one minute at a time.

Here is one trading day. The line is the price of the index, minute by minute, from the bell at 09:15 to the close at 15:30. The shaded box is the opening range — the highest and lowest price the market touched in its first fifteen minutes.

That box is the only thing the algorithm cares about. As long as price stays inside it, nothing happens. The moment a candle closes outside it, the algorithm fires.

Drag the marker below to step through the day, minute by minute. Watch the signal come to life.

II. What the algo can't see

Range breakouts fail more often than they succeed — that's not a flaw, that's the market. What makes the algorithm worth running is not the win rate but the asymmetry: when it wins, it wins big; when it loses, the stop is one range-width away. A losing day is small. A winning day is a meaningful one.
