HURRICANE / 06 INSTRUMENTS WHAT THE HUNTERS CARRY
// What the hunters carry

Five instruments.
That's the whole kit.

  • Time horizon. The one advantage retail has over institutions. Compounding works when you own decades — not quarters.
  • Diversification. Spread across 50+ stocks via an index. Not 5 tickers someone hyped on YouTube last Tuesday.
  • SIP. Monthly buys, automated, same date. Let the market time you.
  • Index core. 70% of your equity in a broad index — Nifty 50, Nifty Next 50, or total-market. The core nobody can fire you from.
  • Rebalance. Once a year. Trim winners, top up losers. Boring. Effective. Two hours of work for one of the biggest wins you'll book.
06INSTRUMENTS
HURRICANE / 07 TRAPS WHAT THE HUNTERS DON'T DO
// What the hunters don't do

Five traps.
Most portfolios die
in one of these.

  • Market timing. Miss the 10 best days of a decade → half your long-run return gone. You won't be right more than wrong.
  • Gurus. Anyone with a target price and no skin in your outcome is selling you dreams. That includes the YouTuber with the PowerPoint.
  • FOMO. The trend you're hearing about is two months old. By the time it hits your feed, the early money is already getting out.
  • F&O without a system. Leverage magnifies your worst month. Without written rules, you will find it.
  • All-in on one stock. The stock you love will betray you. Every era has a Yes Bank, a DHFL, a Vodafone-Idea.

14FEE AT SCALE
HURRICANE / 15 THE CAGE SEBI MF REGULATIONS 1996 · SCHEDULE VII
// The rule that forbids fortunes

Mutual funds are forbidden from
the one thing that builds fortunes.

SEBI MUTUAL FUND REGULATIONS · SCHEDULE VII · RULE 10(B)

No equity scheme may hold more than 10% of NAV in any single stock. If a holding appreciates past the cap, the manager has ~30 days to TRIM IT BACK.

Designed to protect retail from concentration. Side-effect: forced selling of every compounding winner.

THE MILLIONAIRE'S PLAYBOOK
50%
Buffett · Apple. Peaked at ~50% of Berkshire. He held.
80%
Bezos · Amazon. 30+ years at 80%+ of net worth.
90%+
Musk · Tesla. Same pattern.
∞
Dhirubhai · Reliance. Concentrated for generations.

FORTUNES ARE BUILT BY NOT TRIMMING THE WINNERS.

THE MUTUAL FUND CAGE

Stock goes from 6% → 12% of the fund. Manager is legally required to sell.

Must trim your biggest winner — every time it wins — and buy something that isn't.

A Buffett-style concentrated position is illegal inside a mutual fund. By design.

DESIGN: PROTECT RETAIL FROM CONCENTRATION.
SIDE-EFFECT: FORBIDS THE THING THAT BUILDS OUTSIZED RETURNS.

INDEX FUNDS DON'T HAVE THIS PROBLEM.   They let winners run to whatever weight the market gives them. TCS at 11% of Nifty? It stays at 11%.

THE FIRM

DALBAR, INC.

Boston-based research firm. Independent. Has audited mutual-fund investor returns since 1994 — 32 years of data.

THE STUDY

QAIB

QUANTITATIVE ANALYSIS OF INVESTOR BEHAVIOR

Annual. Covers the entire US mutual-fund industry — not a sample. Aggregate cash-flow data from ICI.

THE METHOD

FOLLOW THE MONEY

Compute an IRR from every rupee flowing in and out of equity funds — month by month. That's what the average investor actually earned.

WHAT THEY COMPARE

What the market gave.
S&P 500 total return — buy-and-hold, no trading, no emotion.

What investors took home.
IRR of the aggregate investor, after every buy, every sell, every panic.

The difference isn't fees (already netted out of NAV). Isn't tax. It's when investors put money in and took it out. Pure timing.

Methodology is debated — Morningstar's Mind the Gap and Vanguard find smaller gaps (1.5–2%) using time-weighted math. But every serious study finds a gap in the same direction. Only the size is contested.
